Admissions

Neurodiagnostic Technology Institute is open to any qualified student who has a genuine desire to join the allied healthcare field. No qualified person will be excluded from enrollment based on age, race, gender, disability or national origin. Applications must be submitted by the application deadlines.
Online Course Requirements

To be qualified for the online courses of the Neurodiagnostic Technology Institute program you must meet the following requirements:

  1. You must be at least eighteen (18) years old; or, if under the age of 18, a parent or guardian must sign the Enrollment Agreement. All applicants must be at least 18 years old prior to beginning the first course.
  2. You must provide proof of high school diploma, high school certification, or GED.
  3. You must provide your academic transcripts for the highest level of formal education achieved.
  4. You must provide valid government issued photo identification.
  5. You must complete and submit the Application for Admission and fee.
  6. You must score at least 80% on a basic skills assessment that includes math, English comprehension, and computer skills.
Externship Requirements

Your Externship gives you a hands-on learning experience under the supervision of Registered EEG Technologists in approved, local, neurological testing facilities. The Institute will help place you in an learning environment which could be located in a hospital, clinic, or a physicianâ€™s office. Externship locations have varying needs for health certifications depending on the types of patients they serve. You may be asked to provide some or all of the following documents:

  1. Provide a Certificate of Health form signed by a physician which verifies you are/have the following:
    1. The ability to walk and stand for long periods of time.
    2. Above-average manual dexterity and hand/eye coordination.
    3. Good vision and hearing (or correctable to good).
    4. The ability to lift 40 lbs.
    5. The physical capable to work within a hospital environment.
    6. Pregnant students must also obtain a separate health declaration letter signed by an obstetrician.
  2. Provide proof of health insurance.
  3. Provide proof of certification in Basic Life Support for Healthcare Providers. Information about courses in your area can be found on the American Heart Association website or by contacting your local hospital.
  4. Provide proof of current vaccinations.
  5. Provide a negative drug screening.
  6. Pass a background check.

These admission requirements are for your protection and the protection of the patients and employees of the hospitals, clinics, and private practices where you will be learning. Your application will be reviewed once all documents for the Online Course Requirements have been met. The Institute will work with you to identify an approved clinical site for your externship and will help you collect the documents needed to fulfill the Externship Requirements.
